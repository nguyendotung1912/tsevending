# Vietnam's Smart Locker Market in 2026: What 10 Years in the Field Taught Me

*By Nguyen Do Tung, Co-founder of TSE Vending*

---

When I started TSE Vending in 2014, most people I met had never seen a vending machine that didn't dispense cigarettes or soft drinks from a coin slot. Smart lockers — the kind that receive packages, store office supplies, and integrate with mobile payments — were a foreign concept to the Vietnamese market.

Twelve years later, I'm writing this from our office in Ho Chi Minh City, where we've deployed hundreds of smart locker and vending machine systems across Vietnam. Here's what I've learned about this market that you won't read in a market research report.

## The Market Is Bigger Than the Numbers Suggest

Global research firms cite Vietnam's vending machine market at $X million, growing at Y% annually. These numbers miss the real story.

Vietnam has two parallel markets happening simultaneously:

**Market 1: Corporate/institutional buyers** — Building management companies, factories, hospitals, and universities that want to automate services without hiring more staff. This market is sophisticated, price-sensitive, and values long-term service relationships above everything else.

**Market 2: Individual entrepreneurs** — People who want to place a vending machine in a coffee shop or apartment lobby and earn passive income. This market is enormous, often underestimated, and has completely different needs.

Most competitors focus on one or the other. The companies that win serve both.

## Why Smart Lockers Are Growing Faster Than Vending Machines Right Now

If you ask me which product category I'd bet on for the next three years, it's smart lockers — specifically parcel lockers for residential apartments and last-mile delivery.

Here's why:

**E-commerce growth created the problem.** Vietnam's e-commerce sector grew dramatically post-pandemic. Every apartment building now receives dozens of packages daily. The old system — security guards signing for packages and calling residents — doesn't scale.

**Residents are demanding the solution.** When we pitch to apartment management companies, we rarely need to explain the problem. They already know it. We just need to show them the ROI.

**The competition is still early.** Unlike vending machines (where you compete against imports from China selling at razor-thin margins), the smart locker space in Vietnam still has room for local players who understand the market deeply.

## The Three Biggest Mistakes I See New Entrants Make

**Mistake 1: Treating it as a hardware sale.**

Smart lockers are a service business disguised as hardware. The machine is just the entry point. The real product is uptime, software reliability, and technical support. We've won contracts not because we had the cheapest hardware, but because we could prove our systems run at 99.5%+ uptime with 24-hour response times.

**Mistake 2: Ignoring payment diversity.**

Vietnam's payment landscape is fragmented: VNPay, Momo, ZaloPay, bank transfers, and yes — still cash in many locations. Any smart locker that only supports one payment method will fail in the field. We learned this when our first generation of lockers was QR-only and users in District 12 complained they couldn't use them.

**Mistake 3: Underestimating the installation complexity.**

A smart locker needs reliable power, internet connectivity, and physical security. In a 20-floor apartment building, running a dedicated line to the ground floor lobby is a negotiation with building management, an electrician, and sometimes the local fire safety inspector. Budget 3–5x more time for installation than you think you need.

## What Actually Drives Purchase Decisions

After hundreds of sales conversations, the decision usually comes down to three things — in this order:

1. **Can you prove it works?** Reference clients matter more than any spec sheet. If you have 3 locker systems running in similar apartments with similar traffic, show those numbers.

2. **What happens when it breaks?** Buyers ask this in every single meeting. The honest answer: things will break. What matters is your SLA. We promise a 24-hour response time for critical failures (system down) and 72 hours for minor issues (one compartment stuck).

3. **Is the price fair?** Not "is it cheap" — is it fair for the value delivered. Vietnamese B2B buyers are sophisticated. They've been burned by cheap imports that required expensive local repairs.

## Looking Forward

The next major shift I see coming is integration with building management systems. Right now, smart lockers are standalone devices. The next generation will be connected to the building's access control, electricity management, and resident apps.

When a package arrives, the locker knows which resident it belongs to, sends a push notification through the building app, and logs the delivery time automatically. When the resident picks up, the system records it. Building management gets full visibility without any manual work.

This is where [TSE Vending](https://tsevending.com) is focusing our R&D for 2026–2027. If you're working on building management software and want to explore integration, I'd love to connect.

## Final Thought

Vietnam's smart locker market is not yet mature, which means it's still possible to build a defensible position with genuine expertise and good service. The window for that won't be open forever.
